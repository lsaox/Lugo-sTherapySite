Anxiety

Relief from Anxiety

Anxiety is the feeling of fear or distress in response to real or perceived threats. Everyone encounters occasional anxiety. However, when intense and uncontrollable worry affects your health and well-being, it’s critical to seek support.

Signs and Symptoms of Anxiety

  • Nervousness or Fearfulness
  • Difficulty Controlling Worry
  • Insomnia
  • Sense of Impending Doom
  • Difficulty Concentrating
  • Gastrointestinal Problems

Our providers at Anchor of Hope are experienced in treating various anxiety disorders, including:

  • • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • • Specific Phobias
  • • Panic Disorder
  • • Generalized Anxiety Disorder
  • • Separation Anxiety Disorder
If anxiety is hindering your performance at school, work, or relationships, our team is here to help.

Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety is a normal and often healthy emotion characterized by feelings of unease, worry, or fear about something that is about to happen or might happen in the future. It is a natural response to stress or a perceived threat, and it can help individuals cope with challenging situations. However, when anxiety becomes excessive, overwhelming, or persistent, it can interfere with daily activities, relationships, and overall well-being.

Anxiety Treatment

  • Whether your anxiety is mild or severe, you don't have to suffer alone. At Anchor of Hope, we're here to provide the life-transformative care you need to feel good again. Treatment options for anxiety typically include medications and psychotherapy, with many individuals benefiting from a combination of both. Psychiatrists at Anchor of Hope can discuss medication options, while our team employs cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) to assist children, adolescents, and adults in managing anxiety.

To create an individualized plan, you need the right specialist who can pinpoint symptoms and understand how they affect you or your loved one’s daily life.
